The Blowfish algorithm is the base for secure passwords from online and offline attacks.
An online attack is done when somebody tries to authenticate your password using each guess of it.
On offline attacks, the attacker gets an information (such as a number or a letter) of your password, allowing him to check password's guesses without any authentication system.

The idea of the Blowfish algorithm is to make very difficult to any attacker to guess any part of your password.

As an example: If the user was born on 1968, the probability that the number "68" is on the password is high. On the same way, the probability of having "68" as the two last characters of the password is even higher. Having those two knowledges, an attacker possibilities to hack your password will be increasing.

Many web pages (including banks) demands passwords with 8 character, at least one number and an upper-case letter. So, if your name is John and you were born on 1968, a horrible, horrible password would be "John1968".

Upgrading "John1968"

The idea of a safe password (no matter the length) is not leaving traces of personal information. Also, as Edward Snowden said on the video showed, we need to migrate from a password to a passphrase.

Following the John example, passing from "John1968" to "MyNameIsJohnAndIWasBornOn1968" makes an attacker more difficult to enter into your accounts. The increase of length, from 8 characters to 29 characters is an important element to make a safer web account. Also, elements like having several upper-case letters after the first character makes it stronger. On the other hand, we remain having "1968" at the end of it.

A good idea would be then to distort the passphrase until we have got something useful.
Let's take "MyNameIsJohnAndIWasBornOn1968". The two things we need to transform is the word "John" and "1968".
Taking numbers as letters can be useful. Thus, we can set a "1" as an "I", or an "8" as a "b". deleting upper-case "J" may be a good idea, as the upper-case case letter of our first name can be very current in our thinking. We can maybe use "9" or "6" as the replacement of "J"
The result could be "MyName1s9o6nAndIWas8orn0n". This seems way more undescifrable in comparison to the last one. The problem is that we still have some words which can be descifred, such as "MyName" or "AndIWas".
Of course, if the authentication system allows it, we could also use symbols like "&" or "?". We can then use them to have a more complicated phrase. Using "&" as "And", "?" as "Name" and "$" as "Was" we obtain
"My?1s9o6n&I$8orn0n".
We now have a passphrase which you can read (because you know what it means) and does not have explicit information about John.
